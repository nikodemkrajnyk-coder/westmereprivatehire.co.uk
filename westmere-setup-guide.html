01
Domain & Hosting
Put your website online at westmereprivatehire.co.uk — completely free
Good news. You already own the domain. Hosting on GitHub Pages is completely free. This step just connects the two together.
1
Create a GitHub account (if you don't have one)
Go to github.com and sign up for free. Use your business email address.
Create GitHub Account →
2
Create a new repository
Click the green "New" button. Name it exactly: westmere-private-hire. Set it to Public. Click "Create repository".
3
Upload your website files
Click "uploading an existing file" on the repository page. Upload all these files:
westmere-booking.html
westmere-account.html
westmere-admin.html
westmere-job-email.html
Then click "Commit changes" at the bottom.
4
Enable GitHub Pages
Go to Settings → Pages in your repository. Under "Source" select main branch. Click Save. Your site will be live at:

https://yourusername.github.io/westmere-private-hire/westmere-booking.html
5
Connect your domain westmereprivatehire.co.uk
In GitHub Pages settings, under "Custom domain" type: westmereprivatehire.co.uk

Then log in to wherever you bought your domain (GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc.) and add these DNS records:
# Add these 4 A records pointing to GitHub: Type: A | Name: @ | Value: 185.199.108.153
Type: A | Name: @ | Value: 185.199.109.153
Type: A | Name: @ | Value: 185.199.110.153
Type: A | Name: @ | Value: 185.199.111.153

# Also add this CNAME for www: Type: CNAME | Name: www | Value: yourusername.github.io
DNS changes take up to 24 hours to work. After that, your site will be live at westmereprivatehire.co.uk
Where did you buy your domain? If you're not sure where to change DNS settings, check your email for a receipt from when you bought the domain. That company's website will have a DNS settings page.

Stripe — Card Payments
Accept card payments from customers · 1.4% + 20p per transaction · No monthly fee
Cost reminder: Stripe only charges when a customer actually pays by card. Cash payments cost you nothing. A typical £80 airport fare costs you about £1.32 in Stripe fees.
1
Create a free Stripe account
Go to Stripe and sign up. Use your business name "Westmere Private Hire" and your personal bank account details to receive payments.
Create Stripe Account →
2
Verify your identity
Stripe will ask for your name, address, date of birth and bank account details (sort code and account number). This is required by UK law. Takes about 5 minutes.
3
Get your API keys
Go to Developers → API Keys in your Stripe dashboard. Copy your Publishable key (starts with pk_live_) and paste it below:
4
What happens when connected
✓ Customers can pay by card when booking online
✓ Money lands in your bank account within 2 business days
✓ You see all payments in your Stripe dashboard
✓ Automatic receipts sent to customers
✓ Cash payments still work exactly as before

SendGrid — Automatic Emails
Sends booking confirmations automatically to every customer · Free up to 100 emails/day
1
Create a free SendGrid account
SendGrid is owned by Twilio. Sign up for the free plan — 100 emails per day is more than enough for your operation.
Create SendGrid Account →
2
Verify your sending email address
Go to Settings → Sender Authentication. Click "Verify a Single Sender". Enter info@westemereprivatehire.co.uk as your sender email. SendGrid will send a verification email — click the link in it.
3
Get your API key
Go to Settings → API Keys → Create API Key. Give it a name like "Westmere Bookings". Select "Full Access". Copy the key and paste it below — you only see it once:
4
What emails fire automatically
✓ Customer booking confirmation (with route map, flight tracker link, Add to Calendar)
✓ Your driver job sheet (with Waze links, flight info, passenger details)
✓ Payment receipt when card payment is taken
✓ Monthly invoice to account customers
✓ Reminder email 24 hours before journey
04
Google & Apple Calendar
Every booking appears in both your Google Calendar and Apple Calendar automatically
Both calendars work at the same time. Every booking fires to Google Calendar first. Apple Calendar (on your iPhone/iPad) syncs with Google Calendar automatically — so you see everything in both places.
1
Set up Google Calendar sync
Go to calendar.google.com and sign in with your Google account. Make sure you have a calendar called "Westmere Jobs" — create one if not.
Create Westmere Jobs Calendar →
2
Get your Google Calendar ID
In Google Calendar, click the three dots next to your "Westmere Jobs" calendar → Settings. Scroll down to find "Calendar ID" — it looks like an email address. Paste it below:
3
Connect Apple Calendar on your iPhone
On your iPhone, go to:
Settings → Calendar → Accounts → Add Account → Google

Sign in with the same Google account. Turn on Calendar sync. Done — every booking now appears in your Apple Calendar automatically.

You do not need to do anything else for Apple Calendar. It follows Google Calendar.
4
What each calendar event looks like
Every confirmed booking creates a calendar event with:
✓ Passenger name and mobile number
✓ Full pickup address and destination
✓ Pickup time + estimated journey end time
✓ Flight number (if applicable)
✓ Fare and payment method
✓ Link to Waze navigation for pickup address
